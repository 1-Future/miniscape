# OSRS Boss Mechanics — Exhaustive Reference

## Tier 1: Iconic Endgame

### Zulrah (Solo Boss)
**Why praised:** First rotation-based boss, taught the community prayer switching and positioning
**Kill time:** 1:30-3:00

**Phases (rotates through 4 patterns):**
- **Green (Range)** — attacks with ranged, pray range
- **Blue (Magic)** — attacks with magic, pray mage
- **Red (Melee)** — attacks with melee in melee range, jad phase when ranged
- **Jad phase** — alternates between range and mage attacks, must prayer flick

**Mechanics:**
- Snakelings spawn — small NPCs that attack you, can be killed or ignored
- Toxic clouds — green clouds appear on tiles, deal rapid damage if you stand on them
- Venom — Zulrah applies venom (damage over time that escalates: 6→8→10→12 etc)
- Position-based — must stand on specific tiles based on current rotation
- Gear switching — need to swap between range and mage gear between phases
- 4 fixed rotations — memorize which rotation you're on from the first phase

**Key skill tests:**
- Rotation memorization
- Prayer switching speed
- Gear switching speed
- Positioning awareness
- Venom management

### Vorkath (Solo Boss)
**Why praised:** Consistent money maker, tests multiple mechanics simultaneously
**Kill time:** 1:30-3:00

**Phases:**
- Standard phase — alternates attacks
- Acid phase — spits acid pools, walks back and forth
- Zombified spawn phase — freezes you, spawns undead that must be killed

**Mechanics:**
- **Prayer switching** — alternates mage and range attacks, must switch prayers
- **Dragonfire** — regular dragonfire (need antifire + shield/ward)
- **Acid pools** — green acid on ground, walk between them without stepping on them
- **Acid rapid fire** — spits 25 acid pools then rapid-fires projectiles, must keep moving
- **Zombified spawn** — freezes you in place, spawns an undead creature that walks toward you, must kill it with crumble undead spell before it reaches you (instant kill if it touches you)
- **Vertical fireball** — white fireball that lands on your tile, MUST move off or instant kill
- **Pink dragonfire** — disables prayers temporarily

**Key skill tests:**
- Instant reaction to fireball (move or die)
- Managing acid phase movement
- Crumble undead under pressure (frozen, spawn approaching)
- Consistent prayer switching
- Potion management

### Theatre of Blood (Raids 2 — 3-5 players)
**Why praised:** Best team PVM content, every room is unique, high skill ceiling
**Completion time:** 20-35 min

**Room 1: The Maiden of Sugadinti**
- Blood spawns — red blobs that walk toward Maiden, heal her if they reach her
- Blood splats — AoE blood attacks on ground, must dodge
- Nylocas Matomenos — spawns that must be frozen and killed
- 70/50/30 HP thresholds — mechanics intensify

**Room 2: Pestilent Bloat**
- Walks in a square around the room
- Must attack while it's stunned (falls over periodically)
- Flies — swarm that deals damage if you're in line of sight while walking
- Must hide behind pillars when Bloat is walking
- Stomps — AoE when it falls

**Room 3: Nylocas**
- 3 types: melee (white), range (green), magic (blue)
- Must attack with the CORRECT style or they're immune
- Waves of spawns from 3 lanes
- Pillars — Nylocas climb pillars, must kill them before pillar breaks
- Boss phase — Nylocas Vasilias changes combat style, must switch with it

**Room 4: Sotetseg**
- Two phases alternating
- **Attack phase** — pray against attacks, red ball = split damage with team (everyone stack)
- **Maze phase** — one player is teleported to a dark realm, must navigate a maze while team watches from above and calls directions. Wrong tiles = heavy damage

**Room 5: Xarpus**
- **Exhume phase** — green healing spots appear, must stand on them to prevent healing
- **Acid phase** — Xarpus spits acid, turns ground toxic. Shrinking safe area
- **Screech phase** — stares at a player, everyone must look away (face away from Xarpus) or take massive damage

**Room 6: Verzik Vitur (Final Boss, 3 phases)**
- **P1** — behind a throne, must use dawnbringer special attack. Bouncing bombs between players
- **P2** — walks around room. Purple tornados chase players. Lightning attacks. Nylocas spawns. Electric attacks that bounce between nearby players (spread out)
- **P3** — melee range. Prayer flicking (alternates attacks). Green ball = must be bounced between players (each bounce reduces damage). Yellow pools spawn webs. Verzik heals from webs. Purple tornados continue

**Key skill tests:**
- Team coordination (Maiden freezes, Sotetseg maze calls, Verzik ball bouncing)
- Style switching (Nylocas room)
- Movement precision (Bloat, Xarpus acid)
- Prayer flicking under pressure (Verzik P3)
- Tick-perfect actions

### Chambers of Xeric (Raids 1 — 3-100 players)
**Why praised:** First raid, scalable difficulty, unique room combinations
**Completion time:** 20-45 min

**Combat Rooms:**
- **Tekton** — melee only boss, walks to anvil to heal, must DPS between walks. Hits extremely hard
- **Vasa Nistirio** — teleports players, attacks crystals to heal. Must destroy active crystal
- **Vanguards** — 3 bosses (melee/range/mage) that must be damaged equally (within 33% HP of each other or they heal)
- **Muttadiles** — two crocodile bosses (small then large). Large one eats meat tree to heal, must chop tree
- **Guardians** — two stone guardians, one picks up rocks to throw, must be stunned. Use pickaxe to mine

**Puzzle Rooms:**
- **Ice demon** — light braziers to damage the demon, kindling from trees
- **Thieving** — chest puzzle with poison traps
- **Tightrope** — cross rope while killing rangers trying to knock you off
- **Crabs** — color matching puzzle, smash colored crystals in the right order

**Final Boss: Great Olm (3 phases)**
- **Head and hands** — left hand (mage), right hand (melee), head (main HP pool)
- Must kill both hands before damaging head
- **Left hand specials:** crystal burst (crystals under feet), lightning (chain between players), portals (teleports players)
- **Right hand specials:** healing (green aura, must be clenched), flame wall (wall of fire across room)
- **Head specials:** acid drip (pools on ground), deep burn (fire DoT), crystal bomb (AoE explosion)
- **Final phase** — head only, all specials, acid pools everywhere, prayer switching between mage/range

**Key skill tests:**
- Resource management (potions made during raid)
- Room knowledge (every room has optimal strategy)
- Team coordination (Vanguards balance, Olm running)
- Olm running (taking turns tanking the head, specific movement pattern)
- Prayer switching (Olm final phase)

### The Inferno (Solo Wave Survival — 69 waves)
**Why praised:** Hardest content in the game, Infernal Cape is the ultimate achievement
**Completion time:** 60-120 min (one attempt, no checkpoints)

**Wave Structure (69 waves):**
- Waves 1-8: Basic nibblers
- Waves 9-17: Bats + nibblers
- Waves 18-35: Blobs + bats + nibblers
- Waves 36-49: Melee + range + blobs + bats + nibblers
- Waves 50-66: Mage + melee + range + everything
- Wave 67: 3 Jads simultaneously
- Wave 68: TzKal-Zuk (final boss)

**Enemy Types:**
- **Nibblers** — small, eat pillars (must kill them to preserve safe spots)
- **Bats** — ranged attack, easy
- **Blobs** — split into smaller blobs when killed, must be behind pillar
- **Melee** — runs to you, high damage, must be trapped behind pillar
- **Ranger** — ranged attack, pray range
- **Mager** — magic attack, pray mage, RESURRECTS dead monsters
- **Jad** — 1-hit KO attacks, must prayer flick based on animation (stomp=range, stand=mage)

**Pillar System:**
- 3 pillars in the arena that block line of sight
- Monsters attack pillars, pillars can be destroyed
- Must protect pillars by killing nibblers
- Use pillars to "safe spot" — hide from some monsters while fighting others
- Pillar management is the core skill

**Wave 67: Triple Jad**
- 3 Jads at once
- Each one prayer flicks independently
- Must kill healers on each Jad
- Commonly considered the hardest non-boss wave

**Wave 68-69: TzKal-Zuk**
- Behind a shield (moving wall that blocks damage)
- Must stay behind the shield as it moves left-right
- **Jad spawns** — Jads spawn and must be killed while staying behind shield
- **Set attacks** — Zuk's attacks hit through shield at HP thresholds
- **Healers** — heal Zuk, must be tagged
- **Enrage** — if too slow, shield disappears and Zuk one-shots you

**Key skill tests:**
- Prayer flicking for 60+ minutes straight
- Wave knowledge (knowing what spawns and where)
- Pillar management (which pillar to protect, when to let one die)
- Resource conservation (limited supplies for 69 waves)
- Endurance — one mistake after 90 minutes = start over

### The Colosseum (Solo Wave Arena — 12 waves)
**Why praised:** Newest solo challenge, modifier system adds replayability
**Completion time:** 25-45 min

**Wave Structure:**
- 12 waves of increasingly difficult monsters
- After each wave, choose 1 of 3 modifiers (handicaps)

**Modifier System (Invocations):**
- After each wave, pick a handicap that makes future waves harder
- Examples: "Enemies have +10% accuracy", "Your prayers drain 2x faster", "Enemies heal 5% per hit"
- Higher risk = better rewards
- You CHOOSE your difficulty — gambling that you can handle it

**Enemy Types:**
- Fremennik warriors, archers, mages
- Jaguar warriors (fast, high damage)
- Shaman (poison, spawns)
- Minotaur (charges across arena)
- Seer (magic attacks, can freeze you)

**Final Boss: Sol Heredit**
- 3 phases
- **Melee phase** — must be in melee range, prayer flick based on weapon style
- **Range/Mage phase** — switches between, must prayer switch
- **Grapple mechanic** — Sol grabs you, must click to break free or massive damage
- **Arena hazards** — fire on ground, must dodge
- **Shield mechanic** — Sol raises shield, must use special attack to break it
- **Doom mechanic** — if modifiers are too punishing, Sol enrages faster

**Key skill tests:**
- Risk assessment (which modifier to pick)
- Adaptability (every run is different due to modifier choices)
- Prayer switching (Sol Heredit)
- Resource management under handicaps
- Build planning (gear optimized for chosen modifiers)

## Tier 2: Major Bosses

### Corporeal Beast (Mass/Team Boss)
- **Dark energy core** — bouncing orb that heals Corp and damages players, must dodge
- **Stomp** — if you're under Corp, massive damage
- **Magic attack** — hits all players in area
- **Spec reduction** — must spec Corp with specific weapons to reduce stats first
- **Team coordination** — speccing, tanking rotations

### Nightmare/Phosani's Nightmare (Team/Solo)
- **Totem mechanic** — 4 totems in corners, must be charged by standing on them
- **Parasites** — infects players, must be killed when they burst out
- **Husks** — shadow clones that attack, must kill matching style
- **Sleepwalkers** — walk toward Nightmare, heal if they reach her. Must kill them
- **Grasping hands** — claws from ground, root you in place
- **Flower power** — colored flowers, stand on correct color or heavy damage
- **Prayer draining** — Nightmare steals prayer points

### Nex (40-player mass boss)
- **5 phases** (Smoke, Shadow, Blood, Ice, Zaros)
- **Smoke phase** — virus mechanic, spread between players
- **Shadow phase** — shadow traps on ground, must keep moving
- **Blood phase** — blood sacrifice (heal mechanic), blood reavers that heal Nex
- **Ice phase** — ice prison (breaks free with team help), contain mechanic
- **Zaros phase** — prayer switching, healing from nearby players

### Tombs of Amascut (Raids 3 — 1-8 players)
**Invocation System:**
- Choose difficulty modifiers before starting (like Colosseum but for raid)
- 150+ invocations available
- Target score determines reward tier

**Rooms:**
- **Ba-Ba** — boulder puzzle boss, rolling boulders to dodge, rubble falling
- **Akkha** — shapeshifting boss, memory puzzle, shadow attacks
- **Kephri** — scarab boss, dung puzzle, swarm spawns
- **Zebak** — crocodile boss, blood mechanic, jug puzzle

**Final Boss: Tumeken's Warden (2 phases)**
- **P1** — two wardens (one melee, one range), obelisk charges, energy siphon mechanic
- **P2** — single warden, Zuk-like shield mechanic, lightning, skull attacks

## Tier 3: Popular Mid-Tier Bosses

### Grotesque Guardians
- Two gargoyles on a rooftop
- Must damage both evenly (like Vanguards)
- Lightning attacks — dodge ground indicators
- Prison mechanic — one guardian traps you
- Healing orbs — must intercept before they heal

### Cerberus
- 3 ghost attacks (pray against correct ghost)
- Lava pools on ground
- Triple attack (mage→range→melee in sequence)
- Summoned souls — 3 colored souls, pray in correct order or die

### Alchemical Hydra
- 4 phases (green→blue→red→grey)
- Must lure to correct vent for each phase
- **Green** — poison pools
- **Blue** — lightning tracking attack
- **Red** — fire wall
- **Grey** — jad phase (prayer switching)
- Phase changes at 75%/50%/25% HP

### Gauntlet/Corrupted Gauntlet
- **Preparation phase** — gather resources, craft armor and weapons in limited time
- **Boss: Crystalline Hunllef** — prayer switching, floor tiles change color (must stand on correct color), tornado dodging
- **Corrupted version** — faster, harder, less time to prepare
- Resource management is the entire challenge — no outside items allowed

## Universal Mechanics (Used Across Multiple Bosses)

### Prayer Switching
- Most bosses alternate between combat styles
- Must change protection prayer based on attack animation
- "1-tick prayer flicking" = turning prayer on for 1 game tick when attack lands to save prayer points
- Jad-style = watch animation, switch before hit lands

### Gear Switching
- Swap between melee/range/mage equipment mid-fight
- "Switches" = number of items changed (4-way switch, 7-way switch)
- Higher switches = more DPS but harder to execute

### Tile Movement
- Many attacks target your current tile
- "Pray and move" = switch prayer AND move off tile simultaneously
- "Kiting" = running in patterns to avoid melee bosses
- "Safe spotting" = standing where boss can't reach you

### Tick Manipulation
- Game runs on 0.6 second ticks
- Actions happen at tick boundaries
- "Tick eating" = eating food on the same tick as lethal damage to survive
- "1-tick prayer" = activating prayer for exactly 1 tick

### Resource Management
- Limited food/potions per kill or per raid
- Must balance offense (boost potions) vs defense (food, prayer restore)
- Knowing when to eat vs when to DPS

### Phase Transitions
- Most bosses change behavior at HP thresholds
- Must prepare for new mechanics when boss reaches threshold
- Some transitions are dangerous (Zulrah phase change, Olm phase change)

### Enrage Mechanics
- Some bosses get harder the longer the fight lasts
- Forces efficient DPS — can't just play safe forever
- Creates soft time limits

### Team Mechanics (Raids)
- Role assignments (tank, DPS, runner)
- Split damage mechanics (everyone stack for reduced damage)
- Call-outs (Sotetseg maze, Olm specials)
- Resource sharing
